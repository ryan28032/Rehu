Introduction

**Introduction to the Internet** The internet is a vast, interconnected network that allows devices worldwide to communicate and share information. It connects millions of computers, servers, and devices using standardized protocols. Introduced in the late 20th century, the internet has revolutionized how we access information, communicate, and perform daily tasks. From browsing websites to sending emails, streaming videos, and online shopping, the internet powers countless services. Its infrastructure relies on a combination of physical hardware (like cables and satellites) and software protocols that ensure seamless global connectivity. Today, it is an essential part of modern life, shaping how we live, learn, and work. ...

Main Content

**How the Internet Works** The internet is a global network connecting devices to share information. Here's how it works: 1. IP Address & DNS: Every device has a unique IP address. Domain Name System (DNS) translates website names (like google.com) into IP addresses. 2. Data Packets: Information is broken into small packets that travel through routers to reach their destination. 3. Servers & Clients: Servers store data (websites, files), and clients (your device) request and display it. 4. Protocols: Protocols like HTTP (web browsing) and TCP/IP ensure data is sent and received correctly. 5. Infrastructure: Data travels via cables, satellites, and wireless networks, maintained by ISPs (Internet Service Providers).
